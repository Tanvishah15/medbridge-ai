# Chronic Microvascular Changes (Synthetic Medical Knowledge)

## What are they?
On brain MRI, "chronic microvascular changes" or "small vessel ischemic changes" often refer to minor age- or risk-factor-related changes in small blood vessels of the brain. They are common in older adults and people with vascular risk factors.

## What the report usually means
- **Chronic** = not new or sudden
- **Microvascular** = tiny blood vessels
- **No acute infarct** = no new stroke seen on this scan
- **No mass lesion** = no tumor seen on this scan

## Possible related risk factors (discuss with doctor)
- High blood pressure
- Diabetes
- High cholesterol
- Smoking history

## When to seek urgent care
MRI wording alone is usually not an emergency. Seek urgent care for sudden symptoms such as:
- Weakness on one side of the body
- Slurred speech
- Sudden severe headache
- Loss of vision or confusion
